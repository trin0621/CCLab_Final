Christmas, celebrated on December 25, honors the birth of Jesus Christ and has both religious and cultural significance. Its origins trace back to early Christian traditions, with the first celebration recorded in Rome in 336 AD, likely chosen to coincide with pagan festivals like Saturnalia. Over time, Christmas has evolved into a widely recognized holiday featuring customs such as gift-giving, festive meals, and family gatherings, emphasizing connection, generosity, and the spirit of goodwill that many associate with the season.
